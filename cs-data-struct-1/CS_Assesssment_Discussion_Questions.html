Runtime

  1. When calculating the Big O notation for a particular algorithm, it is necessary to consider the length of time it takes for the algorithm to run as the algorithm's workload approaches infinity. You can think of the workload as the number of tasks required to complete a job. What determines the workload of figuring out whether your box of animal crackers contains an elephant?
    • Answer: Assuming you mean an elephant cracker, O(n), because in theory, you may have to check every single animal cracker in the box to determine whether or not there is an elephant cracker in there. If you meant an actual elephant, it would be O(1), because I'm pretty sure you could tell instantly whether or not there was a live elephant in the box (or it wouldn't be possible for the elephant to fit in the box at all).
  2. Order the following runtimes in descending order of efficiency (that is, fastest runtimes first, slowest last) as n approaches infinity:
    • O(1)
    • O(log n)
    • O(n)
    • O(n log n)
    • O(n2)
    • O(2n)

Stacks and Queues

  1. In the following cases, would a stack or queue be a more appropriate data structure?
    • The process of loading and unloading pallets onto a flatbed truck: Stack
    • Putting bottle caps on bottles of beer as they roll down an assembly line: Queue
    • Calculating the solution to this mathematical expression: 2 + (7 * 4) - (3 / 2): Stack
  2. Describe two more situations where a queue would be an appropriate data structure.
    • A cache, where you discard the oldest information when you are full and need handle new information
    • Checking out customers in line at the grocery store
    • Online merchant processing orders
    • Calls to customer service
    • Directions to a certain location
  3. Describe two more situations where a stack would be an appropriate data structure.
    • Check if the parentheses in an equation are balanced
    • Click undo on your computer
    • Click the back button on a website
    • Eat a stack of pancakes one by one from top to bottom
